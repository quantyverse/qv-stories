"Can't we just do our jobs in peace?" Hank complains, slamming the brakes and hitting the comms module. "Junksters," he spits out. "Listen up, this is our territory today!" he yells hoarsely into the microphone.
A shrill laugh scratches from the soundbar, hurting your ears painfully. "You wish! The planet here is a goldmine for us."
The piercing howl of the targeting lock suddenly cuts through the cockpit. Red warning lights plunge everything into a frantic flashing. Horrified, Hank yanks the steering column around. Beads of sweat drip from his forehead onto the dashboard. Blinding laser bolts hiss mere inches past your cockpit glass, dazzling you entirely.
Instinctively, your fingers fly across the console. You arm the weapons and slam the trigger. The hard recoil presses you deep into your seat. One of your rockets smashes into the enemy ship's wing with a deafening crash, engulfing it in pitch-black smoke.
But the silence lasts only a heartbeat. An ear-shattering crash tears the Janitor apart. A massive block of scrap, hurled by the Junksters, pierces your hull. An icy draft grips you, while fist-sized wood splinters whip past you like shrapnel.
The Janitor plummets unbraked into the atmosphere of X-42-05. The heat presses the air out of your lungs, followed by a brutal impact. The taste of blood and iron floods your mouth. Acrid smoke blinds you.
A wet cough cuts through the crackling of burning planks. Hank lies on the floor, covered in blood and pinned under the massive debris of the control console. Dust clings to his beard. "Damn it, my leg!" he roars frantically against the roaring flames. "Get this heavy wood off me before the tanks blow! Pull that lever, now!"
