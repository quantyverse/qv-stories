With lightning speed, you stumble through the shaking corridor towards the spacesuits. "Damn space moths!" Hank curses, kicking the locker furiously. He holds up his moth-eaten suit, full of holes. "The beasts must like my sweat. You'll have to go outside!"
You grab your intact suit, pull it over your body, and lock the helmet. The breathing air smells artificially metallic. You signal Hank to head front and stabilize the ship.
In the airlock, you hook the safety line and rip the hatch open to the freezing blackness. A hard jerk on your harness, and you are drifting across the exterior hull. The splintered hole in the wood yawns at you. Suddenly, the ship twitches. You slam hard against the planks and almost rush past the leak, as your harness cuts painfully into your ribs. Somehow you manage to press the repair tool against the edges. Super-strong wood proliferates from the nozzle, sealing the gap. Another brutal jerk violently throws the ship to the side.
"Sorry, my bad," Hank's nervous voice scratches through the radio. "Hurry up out there. X-42-05 is dead ahead." You glance over your shoulder. A blinding sun burns your eyes. At your wrist, the oxygen tank's warning signal beeps shrilly and flashes a dangerous red. With a furious hiss, your precious breathable air is escaping through a cracked valve into the cruel vacuum of space.
