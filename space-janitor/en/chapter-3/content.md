After the freezing trip in the void, you rip off your helmet in the cockpit. The air tastes like old wood and machine oil again. You give Hank an appreciative pat on the back, push the throttle forward, and the engines howl deafeningly.
Soon, gigantic shadows block the sunlight – clouds of space junk as far as the eye can see. You head straight into the debris field of X-42-05.
Hank leans on the console. His hands are shaking slightly as he alternates between staring out the window shaking his head and greedily scratching his beard. "How can anyone trash their home like this?" he murmurs hoarsely. "Well... the radar is blinking like a Christmas tree. Throw out the nets!"
You slide over to the side control unit, feeling the cold metal buttons beneath your fingers. With a loud crash, the net launchers fire from the hatches, claw into twisted steel beams, and drag them violently into the Janitor's cargo bay. "Clean shot!" Hank shouts, clapping his hands loudly. "We just need to pulverize that massive block of junk in front of us!"
You grip the targeting system, the crosshairs glowing bright red on the screen. But just before your finger squeezes the trigger, a blinding laser beam flashes outside your ship. The chunk of debris in front of you vanishes in a silent, fiery explosion. A massive shockwave lashes against your hull. Hank stumbles backward, staring wide-eyed at the flickering radar. "What the hell was that?! That wasn't us!"
