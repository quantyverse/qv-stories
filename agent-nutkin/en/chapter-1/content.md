# Chapter 1: Dead Drop in the Oak

The hollow oak smelled of damp bark and old secrets. Agent Nutkin dropped through the entrance hole, landed on all fours, and shook the rain from his fur. The briefing chamber was three knots down — a cramped room carved from heartwood, lit by a single glow-worm lamp.

Director Hazel was already waiting. She sat behind a desk made from a flattened walnut shell, her silver-streaked tail curled tight — never a good sign.

"Nutkin. Sit."

He didn't sit. He never did.

"The Acorn Protocol has been compromised," she said, sliding a bark-parchment across the desk. "Last night, between 0200 and 0300 hours, someone breached Vault Seven and extracted the golden acorn."

Nutkin's whiskers twitched. The golden acorn wasn't just any nut. Inside its shell was a micro-chip — barely the size of a sunflower seed — containing coordinates for every buried weapons cache on the eastern seaboard.

"Security footage?"

"Feathers," Director Hazel said flatly. "Black feathers. Crow feathers."

The Talon Circle. Nutkin had tangled with them before, in the Birdbath Incident of last autumn. They were ruthless, organized, and had a taste for shiny things.

"We intercepted a partial signal before it went dark," the Director continued. "The chip hasn't been transmitted yet. They're moving it physically. You have maybe thirty-six hours before it reaches the Magpie Fence on the other side of the river."

Nutkin picked up the parchment. A rough map. A circle drawn around the east side of the park.

"One more thing," Director Hazel added. "This is off-book. No backup. No extraction team. If you're caught—"

"I know," Nutkin said, already climbing toward the exit. "You've never heard of me."

The rain was heavier now. Nutkin pulled up the collar of his tiny trench coat and disappeared into the canopy.
